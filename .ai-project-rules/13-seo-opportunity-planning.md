## 2. Keyword And Opportunity Tiers

<positive-directives>
- Classify opportunities before planning work: proven win, near-term target, internal radar, or rejected.
- Use GSC query-level and page-level views to find impressions without clicks, positions 11-100, wrong-page matches, and service-location combinations.
- Proven wins can inform customer-facing reports only when ranking proof exists.
- Near-term targets can enter roadmap, page proposals, or preview planning.
- Internal radar terms can trigger monitoring, research, or backlog ideas, but not public claims.
- Rejected terms should record the reason: unsupported service, weak intent, cannibalization risk, insufficient proof, or poor local fit.
</positive-directives>

<conditional-logic>
IF a keyword has impressions but weak average position and no clicks:
THEN treat it as an opportunity signal, not proof.

IF Google shows an existing generic page for a specific query:
THEN treat it as a "testing signal" that may justify a dedicated page brief after service fit, SERP, and uniqueness checks.

IF GSC API sync shows a generic page ranking for a specific service-location query:
THEN create an opportunity brief explaining the mismatch before proposing a new page.

IF GSC API sync is not connected:
THEN keep GSC-mined opportunities in a connection-required state instead of inventing a manual import workflow.

IF a service keyword is not tied to a real customer service:
THEN reject or hold it until the service offering is verified.

IF a market is adjacent to an existing winning cluster:
THEN prefer cluster expansion over isolated page creation.

IF an opportunity is a high-ticket niche service with lower competition:
THEN prioritize it when it has business value, SERP opportunity, and enough proof/content for a unique page.
</conditional-logic>

## 3. Rollout Sequencing

<positive-directives>
- Protect existing proven rankings before expanding into new services or towns.
- Expand first where the site already has topical or geographic trust.
- Prefer high-intent service-location pages over generic city or national pages.
- Sequence work as: evidence -> opportunity brief -> quality gate -> preview -> approval -> deployment -> verification -> monitoring.
- Keep Dach/Spengler core pages safe from cannibalization when adding Hausmeisterservice, Entruempelung, Renovierung, Reinigung, or other service pages.
- Build fewer, stronger, locally specific pages instead of many copied location pages.
</positive-directives>

## 4. Untapped Potential Method

<positive-directives>
- Start with evidence that Google is already testing the domain: impressions, positions 11-100, wrong-page matches, service-location combinations, and nearby corridor queries.
- Validate the business fit before page planning: the service must be real, commercially useful, and operationally supportable.
- Validate the SERP manually for service + city, service + district, problem + city, trade + village, and niche service + location.
- Map opportunities by corridor instead of random towns: existing nearby wins, transit routes, highway corridors, service radius, and adjacent-town clusters should guide expansion.
- Map service niches separately from broad services; high-intent sub-services can be better targets than broad trade keywords.
- Prefer one strong page over several thin pages when the intent overlap is high.
</positive-directives>

## 5. Google Business Profile Support Signals

<positive-directives>
- Treat Google Business Profile reviews as supporting local trust signals, not as direct proof that an organic landing page ranks.
- It is acceptable to create a compliant review-request workflow that shares Google's review link or QR code after a genuine customer experience.
- Ask for honest customer feedback only; do not request a specific rating, keyword, location wording, staff name, or scripted review text.
- Use review monitoring as context for service quality and local trust, not as a substitute for page quality, internal links, schema, GSC sync, or SERP proof.
</positive-directives>

<absolute-constraints>
- DO NOT incentivize reviews with money, discounts, free goods, free services, contests, or other benefits.
- DO NOT ask customers, staff, or vendors to post specific review content.
- DO NOT generate, edit, fake, or keyword-stuff reviews.
- DO NOT present GBP review activity as organic ranking proof.
</absolute-constraints>
